Massage Therapy

Massage therapy is used to help manage a health condition or enhance wellness. It involves manipulating the soft tissues of the body. Massage has been practiced in most cultures, both Eastern and Western, throughout human history, and was one of the earliest tools that people used to try to relieve pain.

I added massage therapy to my studies during the last year of my studies as an intern in becoming a chiropractic intern because I realized that stress, overworking and repeitive movements contributed greatly to my patients' prognoses. Massage therapy can be a great supplement to treatments allowing the focus to be on the muscles and facia while chiropractic care can focus on the movement of the joints.

Acupunture

Instrument Assisted Soft Tissue Therapy

Instrument assisted soft tissue mobilization (IASTM) is a popular treatment for myofascial restriction. IASTM uses specially designed instruments to provide a mobilizing effect to scar tissue and myofascial adhesions.

A stainless steal tool contoured to different anatomical locations of the body. It allows for a deeper application of pressure for soft tissue disorders. The technique evolved from "Gua Sha" in chinese medicine, although the application is slightly different. It works by breaking up scar tissue as well as fascial restrictions caused by surgery, immobilization, repeated strain or other mechanisms. The tool creates microtrauma, thereby stimulating a local inflammatory response and healing.

Athletic Taping

Taping is usually used to help recover from overuse and other injuries. The general goals of athletic taping are to restrict the motion of an injured joint, in order to add stability for a temporary period of time.

Depending on how the tape is applied, the tension created can help to lift up the skin thereby decreasing swelling and pain in the injured area. The tape also provides proprioceptive and sensory feedback for better activation of muscles.

Manual Therapy

In manual therapy, practitioners use their hands to put pressure on muscle tissue and manipulate joints in an attempt to decrease back pain caused by muscle spasm, muscle tension, and joint dysfunction.

This includes soft tissue therapy of tissues (pin and stretch techqniues, massage therapy techniques and mobilizations of joints (low grade, low velocity force applied to joints) to increase mobility of joints when needed

Rehab

The overall goal of rehabilitation is to help you get your abilities back and regain independence. But the specific goals are different for each person. They depend on what caused the problem, whether the cause is ongoing or temporary, which abilities you lost, and how severe the problem is.

A long term relief of pain and avoid re-injury is always the goal. Rehabilization is key in getting to the root of the issue. It's never too early to start the rehab phase.

Spinal Manipulative Therapy (SMT)

SMT was defined as any hands-on therapy directed towards the spine, which includes both manipulation and mobilization, and includes studies from chiropractors, manual therapists, and osteopaths.

A higher grade version of a mobilization, as it is a high velocity, low amplitude thrust that can be applied to any joint in the body (not just the spine) to restore it's normal range motion and aleviate pain.

FAQs

Below you'll find answers to the most common questions for Chiro, Massage Therapy and Acupunture

  • What are adjustments in Chiro?

    Adjustments are high velocity low amplitude thrusts applied to a joint in the body; this includes the joints in the spine, ribs, ankles, wrists to name a few. The purpose is to restore limited range of motion, derease pain, and give a quick stretch to the surrounding muscles thereby decreasing muscle spasms.

  • Do adjustments Hurt in Chiro?

    Adjustments do not hurt, but are a rather a safe and effective way to treat muskuloseketal disorders. This is especially true done by a trained professional after all contraindications ruled out.

  • What can I expect during an initial assessment in Chiro?

    Initial assessments include a thorough history painting a picture of not only the injury but also one's lifestyle and past medical history. To further narrow down a diagnosis, a physical would be conducted which includes range of motion, muscle testing, orthopaedic testing, neurological testing if indicated, and functional assessments. All this information combined, would allow for us to reach our diagnosis(es).

  • I usually get a Massage Therapy when I have aches and pains, do I also need to see a chiropractor?

    Massage therapy is an effective way to help calm the nervous system, decrease tension, improve blood flow, increase range of motion, and speed up healing. Chiropractic initial assessments allow for a more thorough and comprehensive assessment of the problem. Chiropractic care also allows introduction of more treatment moodalities such as rehab therapy, spinal manipulative therapy and referral for imaging when needed.

  • Do the needles hurt acupunture?

    The diameter of acupuncture needles are comparable to a strand of hair; much thinner than a flu shot needle. You may feel a tiny prick as the needle punctures the skin, where all the pain receptors are, but typically patients feel nothing once the needles are in.

  • How does acupuncture work?

    The needles target specific acupuncture points along meridians and anatomical structures of the body. The purpose of the needles are to increase blood flow, inflammatory healing chemicals, the body's natural pain killers to help speed up the healing process.
